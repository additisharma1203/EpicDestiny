It is second largest cave system on the Indian subcontinent.

Belum Caves is located at Belum Village in Kolimigundla Mandal of Nandyal district in State of Andhra Pradesh, India. Kolimigundla is situated 3 km from Belum Caves.

The second largest caves in India after the Krem Liat Prah in Meghalaya and famous for their unique formations such as stalactite and stalagmite formations, the caves were formed over a period of millions of years with the consequent formation of limestone.

At some places, the depth of Belum Caves goes as deep as 46 meters, this point is known as Pataalaganga. An underground stream flows all year at this point, gliding over the rocks and hiding beneath the surface again. The cave consists of various long passages, galleries, large cavities with fresh water and water tunnels. There are three spectacular sinkholes.
